Word of the Day
ephemeral
/ih-FEM-er-uhl/
adjective
📖 Meaning
Lasting for a very short time; transient or fleeting
🗣 Examples
Casual
That viral meme was totally ephemeral—everyone forgot about it in a week.
Formal
The ephemeral nature of success demands constant adaptation and renewal.
Dramatic
Like morning dew on petals, her smile was ephemeral, vanishing before I could capture it.
🔁 Synonyms
fleetingtransientmomentarytemporaryshort-lived
🔄 Antonyms
permanenteternalenduringlastingperpetual
💡 Memory Hook
Think 'ephemeral' sounds like 'temporary'—both describe things that don't last long.
⚠️ Common Mistake: Don't confuse with 'ethereal' (delicate/heavenly). Ephemeral is about TIME, ethereal is about QUALITY.
🌱 Etymology: Greek 'ephemeros' meaning 'lasting only a day' (epi = upon + hemera = day)
